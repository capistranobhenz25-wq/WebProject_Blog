What My Music Preference is And What I Vibe To.

Bhenz Capistrano February 25, 2026 5 min read
← Back to Home

Music isn't just background noise to me, it's the filter through which I experience everything. Bad day? There's a playlist for that. Late night grind? There's a vibe for that too. Here's an honest look at what actually plays through my earphones.

I grew up surrounded by Foreign/OPM Music. From the radio blasting in the kitchen to my relatives' karaoke sessions that lasted until 2 AM, music was always happening around me. That shaped how I hear things. Even now, I catch myself drifting toward lyrics that actually mean something, melodies that feel personal, and beats that hit differently depending on the mood.

OPM — The One That Hits Different

Nothing hits the way an OPM song does when you're Filipino and the lyrics just get you. There's something about hearing your language in a song, the way tagalog flows into the melody, how one line can describe an entire feeling you never knew had words. Artists like Ben&Ben, Dionela, Zack Tabudlo, and Sassa are on repeat constantly for me.

What I love about modern OPM is that it's not afraid to be vulnerable anymore. The new generation of artists write about things that actually matter, heartbreak that doesn't sugarcoat anything, growing up, feeling lost, finding yourself. Ben&Ben especially does this, building entire worlds in their songs with folk guitar and gospel-sized harmonies.

Lo-Fi & Chill — The Study Companion

Lo-fi hip-hop is genuinely underrated as a productivity tool. There's something about those soft, slightly imperfect beats the vinyl crackle, the jazz sample underneath, the rain in the background that creates a bubble around you. When I need to focus on schoolwork, I always go back to lo-fi. It's like my brain's "do not disturb" mode.

  • Lo-fi is my default background when writing or doing assignments
  • I discovered most of my favorite OPM artists through YouTube recommendations
  • Game OSTs (especially from RPGs) hit differently — they're made to keep you immersed for hours
  • I can't study with lyrics — my brain tries to follow the words instead of my textbook
  • Rainy weather + OPM sadcore = the Filipino emotional experience in full effect

At the end of the day, music for me isn't about being cool or having the right taste. It's about what actually makes me feel something. And honestly? A well-timed Filipino song on a quiet night does more for me than any chart-topping hit ever could.

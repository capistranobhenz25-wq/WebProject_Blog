What Foods I Like and Why.

Bhenz Capistrano February 25, 2026 6 min read
← Back to Home

I'm not a chef. I'm not a foodie with an Instagram account full of plated aesthetic shots. I'm just a guy who knows what hits, what doesn't, and has very specific opinions about rice.

Food for me has always been tied to memory and comfort. Growing up, the best times were always around a table, spaghetti on birthdays, adobo simmering in the kitchen on slow weekends, and merienda that somehow always appeared exactly when you needed it. That connection never goes away.

Filipino Food — The Foundation

Let's get something straight: Filipino food is wildly underrated on the global stage, and that's actually fine because it just means more for us. The balance of sour, salty, and savory in Filipino cooking is something I genuinely can't replicate when I try to eat other cuisines exclusively for too long. My body just starts sending signals.

Adobo is the undisputed king. Not even debating it. The way garlic, vinegar, soy sauce, and peppercorns meld together is something I don't think any other cuisine has quite figured out. And the best part? It gets better the next day. Leftover adobo rice in the morning is honestly top 5 eating experiences for me.

The Spice Tolerance Situation

I should be honest: I have a complicated relationship with spicy food. I want to be the person who casually eats laing and Bicol express without flinching. I am not that person. Yet. I'm in training. My current spice tolerance is somewhere between "this is warm" and "water is no longer helping."

Drinks — What I Actually Reach For

I don't drink coffee. Before you close the tab, I know, I know. But the bitter thing just doesn't work for me. Instead, I'm a dedicated iced tea and softdrink person, with occasional visits to milk tea territory when the occasion calls for it. Calamansi juice is severely underrated internationally and I will die on that hill.

🍋 Calamansi Juice 🥤 Iced Tea (classic) 💧 Softdrinks (Coke) 🥛 Ice Cold Milo

Junk Food Confessions

Alright, I'll be real. A significant portion of my eating is just snacks. Chippy, Nova, Piattos, Boy Bawang, these are comfort items. Late night cramming session? Nova. Bored at home? Chippy. There's no shame in the Filipino snack hall of fame. We built it together.

  • Rice is mandatory. Non-negotiable. The meal is incomplete without it.
  • Leftover adobo the next morning > freshly cooked adobo. Science.
  • Merienda at 3–4 PM is a sacred tradition I will defend.
  • Banana ketchup over regular ketchup. Fight me.
  • Leche flan is a dessert, a lifestyle, and a personality.

Look, I'm not here to review Michelin-star restaurants or debate the best ramen in the city. I'm here to tell you that Filipino food, eaten with your family around a crowded table, with rice that's slightly too much, is the best eating experience available to humanity. And that's final.

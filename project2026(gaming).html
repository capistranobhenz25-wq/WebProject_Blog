Bhenz Capistrano February 25, 2026 7 min read
← Back to Home

Gaming is the one hobby that never needed to justify itself to me. No convincing required. Since I could hold a controller or a phone, let's be honest, games have been one of the constants. Here's what actually gets played.

I play on whatever's available usually my phone, sometimes at a computer shop, or a friend's console. The platform doesn't really matter to me. If the game is good, I'll find a way to play it.

Minecraft — The Game That Won't Die (For Me)

I have been playing Minecraft on and off since I was probably too young to be left unsupervised with a pickaxe. And somehow, it keeps pulling me back. There's something almost meditative about survival mode: gathering resources, building a shelter before night falls, digging deeper and deeper into the earth until you've accidentally lost 3 hours.

What makes Minecraft hit different for me is the creative freedom. I'm not a redstone engineer, I'm not building a functional computer inside the game. But I love the feeling of starting from nothing to punching a tree, surviving the first night in a dirt hut, and slowly building something that feels like mine. It's the same reason I get obsessed with sandbox games in general.

Why I Game — The Honest Answer

Beyond just "it's fun," gaming for me is about escape and agency. Real life is full of systems you can't control. Games give you defined rules, clear feedback, and the satisfaction of actually getting better at something in a measurable way. When I hit a difficult boss after 10 attempts, that feeling is real.

  • Minecraft is my "brain off" mode — I can build for 3 hours without thinking about anything stressful
  • MLBB is social first. Half the reason I play is to queue up with friends.
  • Game OSTs have genuinely great music — my Spotify has a lot of game soundtracks
  • I prefer games you can pick up and put down, not ones that punish you for pausing
  • Story-driven games hit differently — a good game narrative can be as good as a book
  • I've probably learned more about history, strategy, and creativity from games than I realize

Gaming gets a bad reputation for being a waste of time, but I genuinely believe the problem isn't games, it's the balance. Games have taught me patience, spatial thinking, and how to handle failure and try again. And Minecraft specifically gave me hours of peaceful, creative time I wouldn't trade for anything.
